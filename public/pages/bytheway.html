ByTheWay
Prototype
 
A high-fidelity prototype for a social media and e-commerce platform targeted to college communities.
Challenge

How might we combine aspects of a social media platform with an e-commerce platform while keeping the design clean and simple?
My Role

UX Design, Interaction Design, Visual Design, Brand Design, Prototyping, Usability Testing
Date

February 2023
Timeline

2 weeks
Tools

Figma, Procreate
01 Understand
Context
ByTheWay is a social media startup which integrates its platform with e-commerce. Targeted towards college campuses, ByTheWay hopes to bring communities to life visually through a variety of formats including photo, video, text, and interaction.
Goal
Our team's main goal was to figure out how to include all the elements which the founders wanted to incorporate into the app while making sure the visuals are clean and friendly.
02 Explore
Organize
We determined the page content for each nav bar icon early on. Since I did not want to overcrowd the nav bar, I combined various elements to the same page.
Loading Animation
Our clients wanted a loading animation which incorporated 8 dots to represent a community taking you in. After multiple trials, I created this, with the dots expanding, but also incorporating the loading animation everyone knows and loves:
Planning User Flows
WIP
Visual + Interaction Design
The visual design we went for was clean and simple, keep a light grayscale with a bold blue accent. I added a slight curve to each rectangular element to lessen the sharp lines and keep the app welcoming and friendly,

The main interactive element was the explore page, where our clients wanted the user to be able to scroll around and explore the bubbles in a 360 format. This would be difficult to prototype on Figma, so we tabled that idea and changed it to a horizontal scroll instead.
03 Finalizing
Iterations + the Final Prototype
We worked with our clients through 3 rounds of feedback, where we were able to quickly address their concerns and add pages as necessary. After the final edits, we polished the grouping organization and made the design pixel-perfect for the prototype.
